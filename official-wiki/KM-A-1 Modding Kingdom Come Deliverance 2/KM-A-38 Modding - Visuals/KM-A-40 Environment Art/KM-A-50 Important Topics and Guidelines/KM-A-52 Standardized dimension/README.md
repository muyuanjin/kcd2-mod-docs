# Standardized dimension
## **General**

There are multiple reasons why we have to keep some dimensions standardized. Some of them are as follows:

* Animations are counting on some dimensions of things they are "interacting" with. (Ladders, chairs...)
* Character collisions are of static size and need to fit through certain assets. (Doors, rooms...)
* We are reusing pre-made assets of fixed sizes and we need to be sure they always fit where they are supposed to.

## **Buildings**

**Floor heights:
225     250     300     350     400 cm**
It is measured from the surface of one floor to the surface of a floor above it. It is important to keep these heights so that we are able to reuse pre-made stairs assets.

**Wall thickness:**

**10     25     60     100     150     200 cm**
It is measured from one side of the wall to the other. It is important to keep these thicknesses so that we are able to reuse pre-made door and window frames.

25 cm – half-timbered and timbered walls (basically derived from the thickness of the wooden beam)

60 cm – typical stony city house walls
